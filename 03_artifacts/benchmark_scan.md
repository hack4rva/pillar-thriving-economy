## Richmond-Adjacent: Procurement Matching & MBE Tools

**1. Chicago City Colleges — M/WBE Action Plan (Euna procurement)**
- What: Centralized e-bidding with participation goals and certification reciprocity acceptance
- Who: Minority/Women businesses engaging CCC procurement
- Why: Lowers search/notification friction; increases credible participation
- Pattern: Centralized notifications; certification reciprocity; standard subcontracting LOIs
- Type: policy | workflow | product
- Links: https://www.ccc.edu/departments/procurement/ · https://www.ccc.edu/services/mwbe-information-and-forms/

**2. Fort Worth — Bonfire Procurement Portal**
- What: Vendor-targeted notifications via NAICS/NIGP code selections; streamlined bid access
- Who: Small vendors seeking relevant solicitations
- Why: Reduces search costs; opportunities pushed to vendors based on saved preferences
- Pattern: Code-based targeting, saved preferences, alerting
- Type: product | workflow
- Links: https://www.fortworthtexas.gov/departments/finance/purchasing/online-bidding-platform

**3. Washington D.C. — Certified Business Directory (DSLBD)**
- What: Real-time searchable directory of certified firms with NIGP code profiles
- Who: Primes seeking certified subcontractors; certified firms seeking visibility
- Why: Code-based matching; directory states it is updated in real time
- Pattern: NIGP/NAICS code tagging, real-time updates, public searchability
- Type: product
- Links: https://dslbd.dc.gov/service/find-certified-companies

**4. NYC — Certified M/WBE/LBE/EBE Directory**
- What: Directory of certified firms (M/WBE, LBE, EBE) with profile search
- Who: Primes, agencies, and certified firms
- Why: High-volume city with mature certification ecosystem — good pattern reference
- Pattern: Multi-tier certification types in one searchable interface
- Type: product
- Links: https://sbsconnect.nyc.gov/certification-directory-search/

**5. San Francisco — LBE Certified Vendor Search**
- What: Directory search for locally certified businesses
- Who: Primes and city buyers seeking certified vendors
- Why: Clean, simple interface for a mature LBE program
- Pattern: Lightweight search over certified vendor list
- Type: product
- Links: https://www.sf.gov/find-certified-lbe-work

**6. Los Angeles — DBE/MBE/WBE Certification Listings**
- What: Searchable table of certified firms by type
- Who: Primes, agencies
- Why: Large-city benchmark for simple directory exposure
- Pattern: Flat table with filtering by certification type
- Type: product
- Links: https://bca.lacity.gov/CertificationListings/DBEtable.php

## Business Setup Navigation Tools

**7. NYC — Business Permit & License Routing Wizard**
- What: Questionnaire that outputs required City/State/Federal licenses and permits for a given business type
- Who: Entrepreneurs starting or expanding a business in NYC
- Why: Reduces "I didn't know I needed that" failures; covers multi-agency requirements in one flow
- Pattern: Decision-tree intake → curated permit checklist output
- Type: product | workflow
- Links: https://nyc-business.nyc.gov/nycbusiness/wizard

**8. Philadelphia — eCLIPSE Permitting System**
- What: Apply, pay, and link licenses across departments in one system; schedule inspections; request multi-department approval
- Who: Applicants across zoning / licensing / permits
- Why: Clarifies sequence and department handoffs; reduces "go to three different offices" friction
- Pattern: Unified intake, cross-department routing, status tracking
- Type: product | workflow
- Links: https://www.phila.gov/services/permits-violations-licenses/get-a-license/use-eclipse-to-apply-for-licenses/ · https://www.phila.gov/departments/department-of-licenses-and-inspections/permits-and-certificates/help-using-eclipse/

**9. San Francisco — Plain-Language "Start a Business" Step-by-Step Guide**
- What: Sequential plain-language guide covering entity formation through licensing
- Who: First-time entrepreneurs
- Why: Reduces cognitive load with clear sequencing and plain language
- Pattern: Step-by-step narrative with links at each stage
- Type: content | workflow
- Links: https://www.sf.gov/step-by-step--step-step-guide-starting-business-san-francisco

**10. Boston — Business Permitting Hub**
- What: Centralized hub for starting or growing a business with permit/license routing
- Who: Entrepreneurs and growing businesses
- Why: Single entry point reduces "where do I even start" friction
- Pattern: Topic hub with clear pathways to sub-processes
- Type: product | content
- Links: https://www.boston.gov/boston-permitting/start-or-grow-business

**11. Boston — Plain-Language Business Certificate Instructions**
- What: Step-by-step instructions for applying for a business certificate (DBA equivalent)
- Who: Sole proprietors and partnerships
- Why: Minimal, clear instructions for a commonly confusing first step
- Pattern: Single-process plain-language explainer
- Type: content
- Links: https://www.boston.gov/departments/city-clerk/how-apply-business-certificate

**12. Citizenserve / GovPilot — Small-Business Navigation Platforms**
- What: SaaS platforms for online business licensing, permit routing, and registration/renewal workflows used by municipalities
- Who: Municipalities deploying self-service permitting; applicants using those portals
- Why: Represents the "off-the-shelf" alternative to custom-built tools; shows what's achievable in a portal product
- Pattern: Intake wizard → auto-routing → status tracking
- Type: product | workflow
- Links: https://www.citizenserve.com/municipal-software-solutions/business-licensing-software/ · https://www.govpilot.com/municipal-clerks-software/business-registration
