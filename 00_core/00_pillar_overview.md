# Thriving Economy — Pillar Overview

This repository supports teams working on the **Thriving Economy** pillar of the Richmond Civic Hackathon. It helps you understand the problem space, review scored problem statements, explore public data, and shape realistic weekend MVPs.

---

## What This Pillar Is About

Thriving Economy focuses on helping Richmonders and local businesses participate in and benefit from economic opportunity:

| Focus Area | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Equitable contracting access** | Helping minority-owned businesses find and compete for City contracts |
| **Small business formation** | Reducing friction in registration, licensing, and compliance |
| **Workforce pathways** | Connecting residents to trades and technical careers |
| **Entrepreneurship support** | Surfacing programs, ecosystem resources, and mentorship |

---

## Why It Matters

Economic participation and small business success drive:
- Income and wealth-building opportunities for underserved communities
- Local job creation and retention
- More equitable access to public contracts
- Stronger, more resilient neighborhoods

---

## Prototype Directions That Work

These approaches tend to succeed in a weekend:

- Make **public information easier to understand** (plain language, step-by-step)
- Make **resources easier to find and compare** (search, filtering, side-by-side)
- Make **public data easier to explore** (visualizations, summaries)
- **Reveal actionable patterns** for residents, businesses, and staff
- Help frontline organizations **triage or route** information requests

---

## Prototype Directions That Struggle

> **Avoid these patterns — they typically can't be completed in a weekend:**
>
> - Require policy changes or new legal authority
> - Require live integration with official City systems
> - Make legal, licensing, or contracting eligibility determinations
> - Depend on private or unavailable data
