## Four Questions to Answer Before Building

> **Who is the user?**
> Contractor, entrepreneur, job-seeker, or City staff?

> **What problem are they facing?**
> Be specific — a workflow, not a topic.

> **What data or documents are you using?**
> If you can't name it, your scope isn't locked yet.

> **How will a judge understand the value in 90 seconds?**
> If you can't explain it fast, simplify the scope.

---

## High-Probability Project Types

These work in a weekend:

- **Procurement opportunity translator** — help MBEs find and understand relevant City contracts
- **Plain-language business setup explainer** — turn dense registration steps into clear guidance
- **Workforce / trades pathway explorer** — show realistic paths into skilled trades careers
- **Small business resource map** — surface programs, contacts, and eligibility criteria
- **Staff or nonprofit workflow helper** — reduce friction in a specific internal process

---

## Red Flags (Stop and Reconsider)

> If your prototype requires any of the following, **simplify scope before building:**
>
> - New policy or legal authority
> - Integration with live City systems
> - Private or unavailable data
> - Eligibility or legal determinations
> - More than one weekend to build
