# BlueSky Problem Statements - Thriving Economy

These broader framings are useful for inspiration. Teams should narrow them before building.

## Turning City Data Into Opportunity — 19/27 (Needs work)
How might we use generative technology to analyze Richmond's business, workforce, and contracting data so that opportunities can be surfaced proactively to the people and organizations who need them?

Notes: Spans multiple data domains and audiences; lacks constraints and a clear stakeholder. Consider narrowing to one user and one data domain.

## Rebuilding the Pathway Into the Trades — 22/27 (Strong) ★ Recommended
How might we use technology to help more Richmond residents — especially residents from underrepresented communities — discover, prepare for, and persist in high-opportunity trades and technical careers?

Notes: Clear user and lifecycle; data sources need to be identified. Teams can scope to discover, prepare, or persist.

## A Smarter Front Door for Starting a Business — 19/27 (Needs work)
How might we create a smarter, more supportive front door for starting a business in Richmond so that entrepreneurs can move from idea to operation with greater clarity and confidence?

Notes: Overlaps with targeted Small Business Navigation; refine with specific steps, departments, and constraints.
